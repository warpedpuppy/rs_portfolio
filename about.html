My Background

Originally from Florence, Alabama, I moved to Tennessee as a young adult and attended college at the University of Memphis. I earned both Master's and Bachelor's degrees, each with a focus in Language and Linguistics.

I began teaching College English in 2008, both on campus and online, but I loved computer-mediated instruction so much that I went entirely online in 2011. I am currently a Visiting Professor of English at DeVry University.

During the 13 years I've been teaching online, I have utilized a wide variety of technology and built complimentary skills including:

HTML (13 years)
CSS (13 years)
JavaScript (5 years)

After I began learning JavaScript, my passion for programming skyrocketed. Having several project ideas and wanting to expand my skills to create them, I decided to compliment my career experience with a formal immersive program.

I completed the Full-Stack Web Developer program at Career Foundry in 2021. Additionally, I added to my former degrees a graduate certificate in Instructional Design Technology at the University of Memphis (2021).

I moved back to my home state in 2018, settling in "Rocket City," or Huntsville, Alabama. The local aviation and aerospace industry--including, of course, NASA's Space and Rocket Center--inspired my home page background animation.

When I'm not programming, I enjoy landscape photography, yoga, and salsa dancing, as well as researching various topics and writing creative non-fiction.
